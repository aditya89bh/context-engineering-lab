# Phase 10 summary: robustness and perturbation analysis

Phase 10 stress-tests the work already built. It adds no new strategy, benchmark
family, or primitive; instead it **perturbs** the Phase 8 naturalistic benchmarks
and measures how far each existing strategy falls from its own unperturbed
baseline. The guiding question: *how robust are context-engineering strategies
when a benchmark's assumptions are deliberately stressed?*

## What Phase 10 adds

- A `perturbations` package with a `Perturbation` abstraction, a
  `PerturbedBenchmark` wrapper that flows through the ordinary runner, and a
  registry of built-in perturbations.
- **Injection perturbations**: `distractor-injection` (competing on-topic noise),
  `contradiction-injection` (conflicting on-topic facts), and `stale-amplification`
  (repeated stale / superseded items).
- **Corruption perturbations**: `source-quality-corruption` and
  `salience-corruption`, which distort observable signals toward misleading values
  without touching ground truth.
- **Robustness metrics**: `degradation` (with `_under_noise` / `_under_conflict`
  aliases) and `robustness_score`, plus comparison and aggregation utilities that
  also compute oracle-gap shifts.
- Four **stress groups** (`distractor-stress`, `contradiction-stress`,
  `stale-amplification`, `corruption-stress`), a deterministic Markdown report, and
  a `context-lab run-phase10` command.

See [robustness-benchmarks.md](robustness-benchmarks.md) for the methodology.

## What it does *not* claim

- Ground truth and the oracle ceiling are never perturbed, so degradation isolates
  a strategy's reliance on a stressed signal — not a change in the task.
- Conclusions are specific to the four stress groups, the fixed perturbation
  intensities and counts, and the Phase 8 seeds and budgets — not claims about
  real-world systems or about a strategy in general.
- Corruption is adversarial by construction; it measures worst-case signal reliance,
  not average noise.
- `oracle` strategies are ceilings, not deployable approaches.

## Benchmark-specific reading

Every statement names the strategy, benchmark, perturbation, and metric, and is
regenerated by `context-lab run-phase10`:

- **Contradiction is the sharpest stressor.** Under `contradiction-injection` on the
  `revision-current-truth` benchmark, `temporal->selection` showed the largest
  single degradation (`conflict_selection_rate`, ~0.81) of any group: a content- and
  recency-driven pipeline keeps the on-topic conflicting facts.
- **Salience corruption breaks signal-reliant retention.** Under
  `salience-corruption` on the `support-stale-fix` benchmark, `salience-retention`
  lost essentially all of its `answer_support` (degradation ~1.0, robustness ~0.0),
  while `attention->selection` was unaffected on `stale_selection_rate`
  (degradation 0.0) — the same corruption hits different strategies very
  differently.
- **Distractors hurt content-blind recency most.** Under `distractor-injection` on
  the `email-old-signal` benchmark, `recency` showed the largest `answer_support`
  degradation (~0.25), consistent with its tendency to chase recent on-topic noise.
- **Stale amplification raises stale selection.** Under `stale-amplification` on the
  `memory-log-noisy` benchmark, `retention->selection` showed the largest
  `stale_selection_rate` degradation (~0.40) as repeated superseded items recur and
  crowd the budget.
- **Oracle distance widens under stress.** The oracle gap on the primary metric
  widened most for `salience-retention` (`support-stale-fix`,
  `salience-corruption`, `answer_support`, +1.0) and for `temporal->selection`
  (`revision-current-truth`, `contradiction-injection`, +0.55), confirming that
  perturbations open, rather than close, the gap to the ceiling.
